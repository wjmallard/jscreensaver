# polyominoes — port notes

Port of `polyominoes.c` (Stephen Montgomery-Smith, 2000; xlockmore/xscreensaver) — repeatedly tries to completely tile a rectangle with irregularly-shaped polyomino puzzle pieces, animating a genuine backtracking exact-cover search.

Original: <https://www.jwz.org/xscreensaver/> · source: `xscreensaver-6.15/hacks/polyominoes.c` (~2369 lines, most of it piece DATA). See [[squiral]] for the shared module skeleton and [[penrose]] for the sibling incremental tiler (forced growth there vs. exhaustive backtracking here).

## Algorithm
A real solver, ported faithfully. Each step (`solveOneStep`, = the C's `draw_polyominoes` body) makes **one net decision**:

1. `findBlank` picks the **most constrained** still-blank cell — among the cells of the *smallest* connected blank region (`findSmallestBlankComponent`), the one the fewest pieces can cover (`scorePoint`), with a corner-bias tie-break. A cell nothing can cover scores 0 and is picked first (fail fast → backtrack).
2. It then tries to `attach` a piece so one of its squares lands on that cell, iterating every `(piece, anchor square, transform)` via `nextAttachTry`. On success the step ends (one piece placed). On failure it `detach`es the most-recently-placed piece and keeps trying the next option from where that piece left off — classic depth-first backtracking.

Two pruning predicates (`checkOk`, run after every tentative placement) reject dead branches exactly as the C does: **every blank region's size** must be a multiple of the piece size (or a positive combination, for mixed tetromino+pentomino / pentomino+hexomino puzzles), and the **chessboard black/white balance** of the remaining blanks must stay coverable (`whitesOk`, using each piece's `max_white`). In **identical-piece** puzzles a third device — the `reason_to_not_attach` table — records which already-placed piece blocked an attach, so the backtrack jumps straight past pieces that cannot possibly help (the C's 2001 search improvement), ported verbatim including the bitwise-OR merge as pieces are detached.

When a placement fills the board (`nr_attached === nr_polyominoes`) it sets `wait = 100` (holds the finished tiling ~100 frames), then resumes backtracking to find the *next* solution. Every `cycles` frames a fresh random puzzle is seeded (`initPuzzle`). 14 puzzle families are ported: 5 non-identical (pentomino; one-sided pentomino; one-sided hexomino; pentomino+hexomino; tetromino+pentomino) and 9 identical (pentomino1, hexomino1, heptomino1[rot180], heptomino2, elevenomino1[rot180], dekomino1, octomino1, pentomino2, elevenomino2). The `rot180` puzzles place pieces in symmetric pairs (a piece and its 180° rotation at the opposite corner), also ported verbatim.

All piece tables (`tetromino`, `pentomino`, `hexomino`, the six single-shape templates, and the `make_one_sided_*` splitters) are copied square-for-square from the C, including each piece's `transform_list` and `max_white`.

## Rendering — 3-D dithered bitmap tiles
Faithful port of the C's `create_bitmaps` + `draw_with_bitmaps`. At init the port precomputes **256** `box x box` 1-bit masks, one per 8-neighbour adjacency index `n` (`buildMasks`, transcribed verbatim from `create_bitmaps` ~786-979): the tile **body** is dithered (`HALFBIT`/`TWOTHIRDSBIT`/`THIRDBIT`/`THREEQUARTERSBIT` stipples that blend the colour ~1/3..3/4 with black → the dark 3-D body), the exposed **edges and rounded corners** are solid `SETBIT` walls (→ the bright rim), and the `G`/`T`/`R`/`RT` geometry, the dedup aliasing (a corner bit is redundant with its edge), and the concave-corner stubs are copied exactly. Per cell, `renderBitmaps` computes the adjacency index from the 8 neighbours (lines 1001-1008) and blits the mask into a board-sized pixel buffer (1-bit → the piece's colour, 0-bit → black), paints blanks all-black, then strokes the `border_color` frame (T nested rectangles, lines 1018-1022). `draw_with_bitmaps` itself redraws every filled cell each frame, so the port's full-buffer `putImageData` per step is faithful (the C's `changed_array` delta tracking is dropped — nothing else reads it).

Both bitmap branches are ported. **use3D** (the C's `NRAND(4)`, true 75% of puzzles — the live binary is always fullrandom, `xlockmore.c:518`, so its `--plain` flag is dead) adds the beveled top→bottom shading gradient plus the `THREEQUARTERSBIT` interior "bellybutton", giving raised tiles lit from the top-left. **!use3D** (25%) is the flatter `HALFBIT` body with the same bright walls. `LARGE_BELLYBUTTON` is **not** defined upstream, so the 3-D `#else` path (943-959) is the one taken.

Bitmaps are used only when `box >= 12` (with `box` rounded down to a multiple of 12, as in `init_polyominoes` ~2210). On smaller boxes — the very wide/large boards (one-sided hexomino 72×5 → box 11, the identical octomino 96×26 → box 8) — the port falls back to `renderFlat`, a faithful port of `draw_without_bitmaps`: flat colour rectangles, **white** boundary lines between differing cells, and a white board outline. (The black blank-blank cleanup segments are a no-op under full repaint and are omitted.) The piece colours, layout, board sizing, portrait rotation, and margins all match the C.

Verified vs the live binary at dpr 1 (820×560, matching the live's pixel size) and dpr 2 (1640×1120, what the retina browser renders): the dark-dithered-body + bright-rim 3-D tiles, the flatter !use3D variant, and the coloured border frame all match across many random puzzles/colormaps.

## Deviations from the C
- **3-D dithered bitmap tiles now ported faithfully** (see Rendering) — `create_bitmaps` + `draw_with_bitmaps`, both the use3D and !use3D branches, plus the box<12 `draw_without_bitmaps` fallback. This replaced the earlier flat-colour-cell approximation.
- **Recursive flood fill → explicit stack.** `count_adjacent_blanks` recurses in the C; on the big identical boards (e.g. 96×26 = 2496 cells) that is a 2000+-deep call chain. Ported as an iterative stack — identical result, no stack-overflow risk.
- **`changed_array` dropped**; we full-repaint instead of drawing only the delta. Faithful: `draw_with_bitmaps` itself redraws every filled cell each frame (canvas persists across the wait-hold frames).
- **Mono path dropped.** The C uses a b&w fallback when fewer than 12 colours (`MI_NPIXELS < 12`); the default `ncolors` is 64, so the colour path always runs. `use3D`/`plain` ARE ported (`use3D` = the C's 75% fullrandom choice; `--plain` is the !use3D branch). `--identical` is exposed as a **working** checkbox — the live fullrandom build actually ignores its own `--identical` flag (fullrandom is hard-set, so it rolls `LRAND()&1`); we honour the toggle as the more useful behaviour the xml intends.
- **Units:** `delay` µs (stock 10000 + a measured OVERHEAD — see Timing). `cycles` (Duration) and `ncolors` (Colors) keep the xml ranges. Keypress / `fps` handling dropped — the host owns keys and the meter.
- **No XOR / feedback tricks** are involved, so nothing to emulate there.

## Palette
`polyominoes.c` is an **xlockmore** hack with a `#define SMOOTH_COLORS`, so `xlockmore.c` builds its colormap with **`make_smooth_colormap`** — 2-5 random HSV anchor hues interpolated into a closed loop, with minimum average saturation/value (muted/pastel; often just two anchors → a near-monochrome ramp). Ported via `colormap.js` `makeSmoothColormapRGB(ncolors)` in `buildColors()`. Each piece's colour index is the C's `(perm[i]*ncolors/nr + start) % ncolors` (random per-puzzle permutation + random `start` offset), so neighbours rarely share a hue. Re-rolled per puzzle, exactly as the `penrose` sibling re-rolls its colormap per tiling restart.

This **replaced an earlier vivid `hsl(h,80%,55%)` full-saturation rainbow** — the systemic palette bug. Verified against the live binary across several runs: the live shows muted smooth maps (e.g. dark-red↔tan, green/slate/navy, or a 4-5 anchor muted spread) and the port now reproduces that character and its run-to-run variety. (The C's mono path — fewer than 12 colours → solid black/white tiles — is not reproduced; the default `ncolors` is 64, so the colour path always runs.) The border frame is a random colour from the same map (`border_color = MI_PIXEL(NRAND(MI_NPIXELS))`), re-rolled per puzzle.

## Timing
Stock `*delay: 10000` µs (matches the `.xml`); the port had a by-eye **50000** (5× too slow). The live binary's delay is a sleep *floor*; its real frame is delay + the per-step solver compute (`find_blank`/`score_point`/`attach`/repaint). The `-fps` overlay held the sleep floor at ~10000 µs across boards while fps swung **39-62** with the board's fill level — an emptier board floods/scores more cells (Load ~60% → ~39 fps), a near-full or just-completed board is cheap (Load ~38% → ~62 fps). The typical/modal reading was **58.8 fps** (= 17007 µs/frame = 10000 floor + **7007** compute), so `OVERHEAD = round(1e6/58.8) − 10000 = 7007` and the loop paces on `(config.delay + OVERHEAD)/1000` ms. Verified: the port's active-step (board-update) cadence measures a steady **58.8 steps/s**, matching the live's modal rate; its own `score_point` compute stays under the 17 ms target, so unlike the heavier C build it holds the delay-bound rate rather than dipping on the emptiest boards (it never runs faster than the author's floor). At dpr 2 (1640×1120) the heavier per-pixel tile blit can dip the active rate to ~46 steps/s on the biggest boards, still inside the live's observed **39-62 fps** band; dpr 1 holds the modal 58.8. The finished-tiling hold is `wait = 100` frames (~1.7 s at this pace), as in the C.

## Config
Ranges mirror `hacks/config/polyominoes.xml`: `delay` (Frame rate, live, inverted), `cycles` (Duration, live), `ncolors` (Colors, reinit), `identical` (Identical pieces, reinit). Non-live changes and "Reset to defaults" re-run `initPuzzle()` via `reinit()` (fresh puzzle + cleared canvas); `r` (restart) also re-seeds.

## Correctness self-review
The SOLVER was the stated risk (stall / infinite loop / no restart). Verified four ways:

1. **No infinite loop, per call.** Each `solveOneStep` ends when `done` is set, which happens either by placing one piece or by detaching the whole stack down to empty (`nr_attached === 0 → done = 1`). Every `detach` strictly decreases `nr_attached` and `nrAttachTry` strictly advances the finite `(poly, point, transform)` index, so the loop is bounded — it cannot spin.
2. **Always visible progress; clean restart.** A headless harness drove **12,000 frames** across **both** puzzle families (non-identical, then `identical` flipped on via `reinit()`): **zero throws, zero hangs**, ~5.5 M cell draws, slowest single frame **5 ms** (per-frame work stays bounded even on the 96×26 / 60-distinct-piece boards). After `cycles` frames a brand-new random puzzle is seeded, so a hard board can never dead-end the screen — it keeps showing pieces placed/removed, then resets.
3. **It actually solves.** Forcing the solvable 10×6 and 12×5 pentomino puzzles, the board reached **100% filled** and entered the `wait`-hold (~5,300 no-draw hold-frames over 30,000 → ~50+ full solutions found, each held then backtracked for the next). The chessboard/region pruning and (identical-mode) `reason_to_not_attach` table were exercised without error.
4. **Hardest size doesn't freeze.** The 20×3 pentomino board (only 2 solutions) churns for thousands of steps without completing in a short run — expected — but never throws or stalls, and in the live hack `cycles` reseeds it long before that matters. Visible motion (placements/removals) the whole time.

**Spot-check in the browser:** confirm pieces visibly drop into the rectangle and the search backtracks (pieces removed and retried), that a finished tiling holds briefly before it hunts for another, that a new random puzzle (different shape set / aspect) appears after a while, and that toggling **Identical pieces** switches to the all-same-shape puzzles. Most puzzles render as the C's 3-D dithered bitmap tiles (dark dithered body, bright top-left bevel, coloured border); ~25% use the flatter !use3D HALFBIT body, and the very wide/large boards fall back to flat colour cells with white boundary lines (box < 12). Confirmed at both dpr 1 and dpr 2.
